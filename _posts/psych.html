Research Study

For our research study, we decided on a few things ...

  • Geographical limits of our study: South Bay Area
  • Age range:13 and above

And our first and most important question

"When you consider the idea of death ... what is your first and strongest response? Please only pick on response!" [should probably reverse this list!]

  • Dread
  • fear
  • anxiety
  • sorrow
  • worry
  • apathy
  • resignation
  • relief
  • peace
  • inspiration
  • motivation
  • joy

Can you add to this list?

additional questions:

  1. Do you live in the bay area?
  2. Have long have you lived here <6mo; 6mos-2yrs; >2yrs
  3. Your age? 13-16; 17-21; 22-35; 36-50; 51-125
  4. Are you religious spiritual agnostic athiest ... yes/no
  5. If yes
  6. What is your belief system? Christian / Muslim / Buddhist / Hindu /Jewish / Cubism / other
  7. Has someone close to you died? If so how recently?
  8. Have you ever experienced an illness, injury, or event that threatened your life?
  9. Are you now, or is anyone close to you at a higher than normal risk of dying?

Over the break, continue to do detective work on your first couple years. Learn what kind of time your parents spent with you. Did they play with you a lot? Was one of them away at work a lot? Did you have everything you needed to be comfortable and at peace? Were you the last child or do you have younger siblings? Note: there are NO judgements behind these explorations. The fact that you are all here studying with me means that you are all excellent and admirable hobbits. We are just looking for patterns that may have interesting implications in your life. Be sure to make connections to Erikson's stages in your analysis.
